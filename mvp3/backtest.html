Flat Bet — TP/SL Sessions 2 / 55 Profitable

Flat bet: $1.00 per label per round
TP: +$41 (session ends)
SL: −$50 (session ends)
Breakeven TP rate: 54.9%
Spins: 59,526 continuous
Breakeven formula: with asymmetric TP/SL the required win rate is SL / (TP + SL) = 50 / 91 = 54.9%. Any strategy whose TP rate stays below that burns money regardless of how many sessions run.

Why single DS beats multi-label with flat betting: a lone double-street pays +$5 on a hit and loses only −$1 on a miss (5:1 asymmetry). This creates a high-variance walk that can spike to TP=$41 in a lucky burst. Multi-label strategies cancel variance — most rounds result in near-zero P&L from coverage overlap — so the walk barely moves and slowly drifts toward the SL on the house edge. Fibonacci solves this by escalating bets during losing streaks, which is why the same multi-label strategies flip profitable under that regime.

Continuous Run Analysis 0 / 55 Profitable

⚠
All 55 strategies bankrupt — continuous play cannot overcome the house edge
Running all 59,526 spins in a single uninterrupted pass (no daily TP/SL stops) with Fibonacci progression and 0.1 % equity base bet: every strategy ended with a final balance under $1. The Fibonacci did escalate during losing sequences, but the mathematical house edge of −2.70 % per bet (1 zero on a 37-number wheel) compounds relentlessly and drains all capital over tens of thousands of rounds regardless of bet sizing.
Init: $1,000

Fib max level: 7 (×21 → 2.1% max total bet)

Why it always fails:
House edge = 1/37 ≈ 2.703 % per unit wagered. With an average bet of ~0.2 % of equity per round (across Fibonacci levels), expected loss per round ≈ 0.2% × 2.703% = 0.0054% of equity. Over 59,526 rounds: (1 − 0.000054)^59526 ≈ 0.04 — a 96% expected capital destruction. No progressive system can overcome this; Fibonacci only redistributes when you lose, not whether you lose. The virtual SL prevented runaway escalation but couldn't reverse the long-run negative EV.

Recommendation — How to grow the fund:
The profitability seen in the calendar-day results (11 strategies profitable) comes from the session TP/SL boundary structure, not from the progression system alone. Daily boundaries create an option-like asymmetry: you capture gains each time the session hits TP (57–67 % of days for the best strategies) and cut losses at SL. To grow the fund compoundly while keeping this structure, the correct approach is:

• Keep daily sessions with TP/SL as per strat.md
• Replace the fixed $0.50 base bet with 0.1 % of account equity / n_labels
• As the account grows the bets and TP/SL thresholds grow proportionally — this is true compound growth
• S1-Aggressive at +7 % per session EV × 57.1 % win rate compounds to double the account roughly every 10–15 winning days
